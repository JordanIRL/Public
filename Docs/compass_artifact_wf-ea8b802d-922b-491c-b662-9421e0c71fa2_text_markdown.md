# The Intune admin's mid-2026 operational map

Mid-2026 is a hinge year for Microsoft endpoint management, and the work that matters right now sits at the intersection of three forces: **Microsoft is finishing its absorption of the Intune Suite into Microsoft 365 E3/E5 on July 1, 2026**, **the cloud-native pivot has hardened into Microsoft's only forward-recommended architecture** (Entra-join, Settings Catalog, Cloud PKI, Autopilot device preparation), and **a wave of platform-driven deprecations from Apple, Google, and Microsoft is forcing migrations whether you planned them or not**. The Windows 10 end-of-support cliff was last October; Apple's Declarative Device Management has become the only future for Mac and iOS update enforcement; Google's hardening of Play Integrity lands October 31, 2026; and the Conditional Access "Require approved client app" grant retires in March 2026. The endpoint team that gets through this year cleanly is the one that uses the next six months to retire legacy plumbing, lock in M365 renewal pricing before the July repricing, and rebuild policy authoring on the unified Settings Catalog. The rest of this report walks through what to focus on, in roughly the order most teams should sequence the work.

## The July 1, 2026 licensing reshuffle drives almost every other decision

The single biggest commercial event of the year is Microsoft's restructuring of Intune Suite into Microsoft 365 plans, effective **July 1, 2026**. **Microsoft 365 E3 will absorb Intune Plan 2, Remote Help, and Advanced Endpoint Analytics**, with a list-price increase reported at roughly $3/user/month (~8%). **Microsoft 365 E5 and E7 additionally absorb Endpoint Privilege Management, Microsoft Cloud PKI, and Enterprise App Management**, again with a roughly $3/user/month bump (~5%). The Intune Suite continues to exist as a standalone SKU for customers on other plans. Volume-discount brackets on Enterprise Agreements were already removed November 1, 2025, so every customer now negotiates from list. Renewals that close **before July 1, 2026 lock in current pricing** for the term, which makes the next two months the most consequential procurement window of 2026.

This repricing changes the build-vs-buy math for several adjacent products. **Cloud PKI starts to look obvious for any new tenant** once it's included in E5: NDES, on-prem ADCS, and Application Proxy publishing of SCEP endpoints can all be retired in favor of the cloud-hosted issuing CA with BYOCA (bring-your-own-CA) anchoring under your existing private root. Third-party SCEP-as-a-service (SCEPman), EPM (BeyondTrust, CyberArk EPM), and patch-management (Patch My PC, Scappman) renewals deserve scrutiny — don't sign multi-year extensions without modeling against the included E5 capabilities. Audit your Intune feature usage report monthly between now and July to catch licensing-without-entitlement risk: Remote Help sessions, EPM rules, Tunnel for MAM, Specialty devices, and Advanced Analytics dashboards all need Plan 2 or Suite today.

## Windows 10 ESU hygiene and the cloud-native pivot

Windows 10 ended mainstream support on **October 14, 2025**, and you are now seven months into the **paid Extended Security Updates window**. Year 1 commercial ESU is **$61 USD/device through October 31, 2026**, doubling to ~$122 in Year 2 and ~$244 in Year 3, sold as cumulative perpetual SKUs in CSP (skip Y1 and you still pay for it). The activation flow is not a UI in Intune — you script it via Intune: deploy KB5066791 + KB5072653, push the MAK, then run `slmgr.vbs /ipk` and `/ato` with the published per-year activation IDs. Cloud PCs, AVD session hosts, Azure VMs, and Azure Stack HCI workloads get ESU free for the full three years, and a local W10 device whose user signs into a Windows 365 Cloud PC at least once every 22 days qualifies free as well — up to ten physical devices per user. **Critically, Microsoft stopped providing user-to-device ESU mapping reports after October 15, 2025**; if you have not built your own audit (Intune custom inventory + Log Analytics is the standard pattern), you are flying blind on entitlement.

For the bulk of the fleet, the question is **Windows 11 versioning strategy**. **Win11 24H2 and 25H2 are the strategic baselines** in mid-2026. 25H2 (build 26200, GA September 30, 2025) is delivered as an enablement package over 24H2 and adds Wi-Fi 7 enterprise settings, the `RemoveDefaultMicrosoftStorePackages` ApplicationManagement CSP, and Windows Backup for Organizations. **Avoid 26H1** (GA February 10, 2026) in enterprise — it is mostly an enablement release and notably **does not support hotpatching**. 23H2 Home/Pro reached EOS November 11, 2025; Enterprise/Edu hits November 10, 2026.

On the architecture itself, Microsoft's guidance is now blunt: **"deploying new devices as Microsoft Entra hybrid join devices isn't recommended, including through Windows Autopilot."** Hybrid join is not formally deprecated, but every new-build conversation should be Entra-joined cloud-native. The new **Windows Autopilot device preparation policies (APv2)** are Entra-join-only, require no hardware-hash pre-registration, and now handle Windows 365 Cloud PC automatic-mode provisioning. Classic Autopilot remains the only path for pre-provisioning (white glove), self-deploying mode, and hybrid join, and both coexist permanently — Microsoft has explicitly stated existing classic profiles do not need to be migrated. If you still run hybrid Autopilot, the **Intune Connector for AD older than version 6.2501.2000.5 stopped processing enrollments** in 2025; the replacement runs under a gMSA. There is also a useful preview path for organizations that genuinely need hybrid join during transition: **instant hybrid join via Entra Kerberos** (Windows Server 2025 domain controllers, build 26100.6905+) eliminates the Entra Connect sync wait and the AD FS dependency.

## Hotpatching becomes the default for Windows quality updates

**Windows Autopatch is now native to Intune** — the old feature-activation toggle is gone, and eligibility broadened to include Microsoft 365 Business Premium and Windows 365 Enterprise alongside the E3/E5/F3/A3/A5 set. The big shift this quarter is **hotpatching defaulting to on for eligible devices starting with the May 2026 security update**, with opt-out controls available tenant-wide or per quality-update policy as of April 1, 2026. Hotpatch prerequisites are Win11 24H2 (build 26100.2033+) or 25H2 Enterprise, virtualization-based security enabled, on the current quarterly baseline, and managed by Intune Autopatch. The calendar shifts from 12 reboots per year to **four baseline (restart) months and eight hotpatch months**. ARM64 is in preview; if you opt ARM64 in you must disable CHPE, which can break 32-bit x86 apps — usually a reason to exclude. **26H1 does not support hotpatch, full stop.**

The companion change for L3s is the **Settings Catalog → Windows Update client policies** path replacing the older Devices/Windows Updates blade for canonical Quality Update Policy authoring, with Expedite and Hotpatch toggles in-policy. Driver and firmware policies live separately and **must not be duplicated** — one driver update policy per device or you generate approve/decline conflicts. If you came from WSUS, confirm that Dual Scan is not stranding Autopatch reporting. The Security Baseline for Windows is now at **version 25H2** (released March 2026), the Edge baseline jumped to **v139** in April 2026, and the MDE baseline at 24H1 is still optimized for physical devices — **don't apply it to Cloud PCs or AVD**; use the Windows 365 24H1 baseline there. A perennial trap: the MDE baseline enables TPM startup PIN by default, which silently blocks silent BitLocker enablement. Audit `HKLM\SOFTWARE\Policies\Microsoft\FVE` if encryption is failing on baseline-managed endpoints.

## Apple management is now a forced migration to Declarative Device Management

Apple released iOS/iPadOS 26 and macOS 26 (Tahoe) in fall 2025 with the legacy MDM software-update commands and the `com.apple.SoftwareUpdate` payload deprecated. **Intune ended support for the legacy iOS/iPadOS and macOS update policies starting with Service Release 2602 (week of March 2, 2026)**, and admins are expected to move all update enforcement to the **Declarative Device Management** category in Settings Catalog. The two new DDM-based models are **Software Update Enforce Latest** (delay in days + install time, used for rapid-patch posture) and **Software Update** (target OS/build version + target date, used for strict version pinning during app validation). DDM is asynchronous, device-driven, self-healing, works offline, and reports status proactively over a dedicated status channel — which means status reports finally tell you whether install failures are caused by storage, battery, or actual policy state.

The DDM footprint in Intune is now broad and growing. Passcode, Safari extensions, math/keyboard/Siri settings, disk management on macOS, audio accessory settings, and a new **Intelligence Settings / External Intelligence Settings** category (added in Service Release 2603, week of March 30, 2026) all live under Settings Catalog → Declarative Device Management. The Intelligence category is the governance lever for **Apple Intelligence** — block Genmoji, Image Playground, Writing Tools, Smart Replies, and Mail/Safari/Notes summaries, or force on-device-only dictation and translation. Service Release 2603 also added the option to deliver **required iOS LOB apps via DDM on iOS/iPadOS 18+**, which gives real-time per-app status and faster delivery. **Assignment filters now apply to DDM-based configurations**, so you can do phased rollouts the same way as everything else.

For enrollment, the modern story for corporate-owned Apple is **Setup Assistant with modern authentication + Just-In-Time registration** via the Microsoft Enterprise SSO plug-in. JIT eliminates the Company Portal dependency for Entra registration and compliance, and **Apple Business Manager federated authentication with Entra ID** is effectively a prerequisite for clean Account-Driven User Enrollment for BYOD. The ABM ↔ Entra federation also lets you skip provisioning Managed Apple IDs manually. New Apple enrollments in Intune began using **ACME instead of SCEP** for certificate issuance in January 2026 — no admin UI change but worth knowing for cert-connector audits. ADE tokens still expire annually, so check renewal dates and confirm there's an Apple ID owner who hasn't left the company.

macOS-specifically, **Platform SSO went GA in August 2025** and is the configuration to roll out tenant-wide. The Secure Enclave method is preferred — it provisions a hardware-bound passkey, lets any Entra org user log in at the macOS login window like Windows Hello, and works for shared-Mac scenarios via Create User At Login. The legacy macOS Endpoint Protection device-configuration template is deprecated; **rebuild FileVault, Firewall, and Gatekeeper as Settings Catalog policies** (Full Disk Encryption category includes recovery-key rotation in months and Setup Assistant force-enable for ADE-enrolled devices). A new **Recovery Lock** policy plus rotate-password remote action shipped in Service Release 2603 — set it up alongside macOS LAPS for ADE devices. **Endpoint Privilege Management on macOS is still not GA** as of May 2026; the compensating controls remain macOS LAPS, Platform SSO, and standard-user accounts. Plan for macOS 26 being the final macOS supporting Intel hardware, and watch for new Defender for Endpoint payload requirements that Apple injects at each major version.

## Android: clean up Device Administrator and prepare for Play Integrity tightening

Android management has two operational priorities right now. The first is finishing the **Android Device Administrator legacy cleanup** — Microsoft Intune ended support for DA on GMS devices at the end of 2024, and DA documentation only remains for Android 15 and earlier without GMS. Block new DA enrollments via a platform enrollment restriction and use the "Move Android devices from device administrator to personally-owned work profile management" admin action, or retire and re-enroll into Android Enterprise. For non-GMS hardware (rugged, China, AR/VR), use AOSP enrollment paths; **Android XR was added as a supported enrollment platform in April 2026** in dedicated and fully-managed modes.

The second priority is the **October 31, 2026 Play Integrity enforcement change**. Google has updated the definition of "Strong Integrity" to require hardware-backed signals plus a security patch within the last 12 months, and Intune softens this today for backward compatibility but **will fully enforce on October 31, 2026**. Devices on Android 13+ without a patch in the last 12 months will be downgraded from Strong to Device Integrity, breaking compliance and triggering Conditional Access blocks. The mitigation is to configure App Protection Policy Conditional Launch with Min OS version and Min patch version now, use the app protection status report to surface stale-patch devices, and push users to update before the cutoff. SafetyNet is fully deprecated — Play Integrity is the only signal. The Microsoft Defender for Endpoint on Android app remains the standard threat-defense feed into both compliance and APP conditional launch, and it requires a self-looping VPN for web protection.

Personally-owned work profile enrollment is also moving to Google's **Android Management API (AMAPI)** through 2026, which brings a web-based enrollment flow without app install and modernized policy delivery. Track this in Intune's "In development" page and plan to retest your BYOD onboarding scripts. For frontline and ruggedized devices, **Knox Service Plugin** and Zebra's OEMConfig Powered by MX remain the primary OEMConfig paths — note that non-Zebra devices accept only one OEMConfig policy unless settings belong to different top-parent bundles. Android minimum supported is now **Android 10** for user-based scenarios and Android 8 for userless (dedicated/COSU); only the three most recent OS versions are "supported," everything else is just "allowed."

## Identity, Conditional Access, and the slow death of hybrid join

The endpoint-identity stack is consolidating around a few clear recommendations. **Windows Hello for Business Cloud Kerberos Trust** is the model for hybrid environments — certificate trust is actively discouraged for new deployments. **Synced passkeys in Entra ID went GA in March 2026** and **Entra passkey on Windows went GA in late April 2026** for commercial (GCC/GCC-H/DoD GA in July 2026), stored in the Hello container and provider-restrictable via the new passkey profiles model (up to three profiles, AAGUID allow/block lists, attestation enforced or off). The practical pattern is a tight admin profile (device-bound only, attestation enforced, corporate-issued AAGUIDs and Authenticator) plus a broader user profile (synced + device-bound, no attestation). Enforce passkey use through Conditional Access **authentication strength** rather than per-method toggles.

On the Conditional Access side, three changes deserve attention right now:

- **The "Require approved client app" grant control retires March 2026.** Audit and replace with "Require app protection policy" — many tenants still have stale CA policies relying on the older grant.
- **Token Protection went GA and is now licensed at Entra ID P1** (previously P2-only), expanding to Exchange Online, SharePoint, Teams desktop, and Graph PowerShell SDK on Windows; macOS/iOS coverage is still in preview.
- **CA policy targeting "All resources with resource exclusions" is changing between March 27 and June 2026** to no longer auto-exempt the low-privilege scopes `User.Read`, `openid`, `profile`, `email`, `offline_access`. Apps like Azure CLI that previously bypassed CA when requesting only these scopes will now hit MFA and device requirements — review custom apps and policies before the rollout completes.

Microsoft's **device-identity end state is Entra-joined cloud-native**, with the explicit Learn statement that "Hybrid Microsoft Entra Join shouldn't be long term nor the end goal for any organization." There is no supported in-place conversion from hybrid to Entra-joined; you migrate at hardware refresh, reimage, or troubleshooting events. On the RBAC side, lean on **PIM for the Entra-built-in Intune Administrator role** for full-tenant work and **PIM for Groups** for least-privilege Intune RBAC role assignments (Help Desk Operator, Policy and Profile Manager, Endpoint Security Manager, Endpoint Privilege Manager). The **Intune Scoped Permissions preview** that arrived in Service Release 2603 (March 2026) changes long-standing role-merging behavior — pilot in a non-prod tenant and run the Permissions Assessment Report before enabling tenant-wide. Multi-Admin Approval now covers RBAC changes, app deployments, and scripts.

## Endpoint security: EPM maturity, ASR rules, and Cloud PKI as the new default

**Endpoint Privilege Management** is now mature enough to be the canonical answer for removing local admin rights. The October 2025 (Service Release 2510) **EPM Dashboard** surfaces "users with only unmanaged elevations" (your admin-removal candidates), "frequently approved by support" (your rule candidates), and "frequently denied elevations" — telemetry that lets you build the runbook for: audit two-to-four weeks, auto-create rules from approved requests with file-path pinning in non-writable locations and certificate validation, switch the default response from Deny to Require support approval, then remove users from local Administrators. The new **Elevate as current user** elevation type (October 2025) handles installers that need the user profile and supports MFA. Security Copilot integration adds Defender TI scoring on elevation requests. Two new ASR rules went GA in April 2025 that should be on your audit-to-block path: **Block rebooting machine in Safe Mode** (counters the bcdedit/safeboot ransomware tactic) and **Block use of copied or impersonated system tools** (counters renamed LOLBins). Both target device groups, not user groups.

**Microsoft Cloud PKI** is the right answer for nearly all new certificate scenarios — SCEP via cloud-hosted issuing CAs, no NDES, no Cert Connector, no IIS, HSM-backed keys via Azure Managed HSM, BYOCA for anchoring under an existing private root. Constraints to remember: SCEP only (no PKCS via Cloud PKI), Intune-managed devices only, no OCSP (CRL is HTTP with 7-day validity, 3.5-day refresh), maximum six CAs per tenant, no `Any Purpose` EKU. **In-place CA renewal is in development for 2026**; until then, renewal means a new CA and re-pointing SCEP profiles. Cloud PKI does **not** replace imported PKCS for shared S/MIME decryption certs — keep your existing PKCS pipeline for that scenario. With E5 absorbing Cloud PKI in July 2026, this is the moment to pilot.

**Microsoft Defender for Endpoint integration with Intune** is by now table stakes: connector on, EDR onboarding via Endpoint Security node, Tamper Protection enforced via MDM (block mode on macOS too), EDR in Block Mode for devices in passive AV mode, and Machine Risk Score feeding compliance at the Medium block-threshold as the industry baseline. The new **Admin Tasks node** (Tenant administration, GA February 2026) consolidates MDE security tasks, EPM elevation approvals, and Multi-Admin Approval requests in one place. **Microsoft Defender Vulnerability Management** features moved into the **Exposure Management** view in the Defender portal; security tasks created there flow into Intune for remediation, capped at 10,000 devices per request. Watch for the **Vulnerability Remediation Agent** (Security Copilot) which is now available in tenants with Intune Plan 1 + Defender VM + Security Copilot SCUs.

For non-Windows threat protection, Defender for Endpoint on Mac supports the three most recent macOS versions (26 Tahoe, 15 Sequoia, 14 Sonoma) with offline security intelligence updates GA as of September 2025. Defender on iOS gained Intune certificate inventory sync in Service Release 2601 for Zimperium-backed analysis of installed certs. Linux ARM64 server went GA in April 2025. **RHEL 8 LTS Intune support ends July 2026; Ubuntu 22.04 LTS ends August 2026** — schedule the OS upgrades now.

## Settings Catalog, Remediations, and Graph automation are the new policy plumbing

The unified settings platform has effectively won. **Administrative Templates profile type has been deprecated and read-only since the December 2024 (2412) service release** — existing policies apply but cannot be modified, and you cannot create new ones. The Settings Catalog has an "Administrative Templates" branch mirroring `Computer Configuration\Administrative Templates\…` plus user-side settings tagged "(User)". The macOS Endpoint Protection template is deprecated; Device Features and Device Restrictions are on the migration runway. Microsoft Learn now explicitly recommends: *"create all new policies using the settings catalog where possible."* There is **no first-party migration tool** for legacy Administrative Templates — community converters exist but require careful permissions review.

Targeting hygiene has settled around two principles. **Assignment filters evaluated at check-in are preferred over dynamic device groups** for OS, model, ownership, and enrollment-profile-name targeting (200 filters per tenant, 3,072 chars per rule). Compliance and configuration profiles should usually be **assigned to user groups, then refined by device filters**; mixing user-group exclusions with device-group includes is unsupported. Use the built-in virtual `All users` and `All devices` rather than custom catch-all groups to avoid Entra sync churn — important if you're preparing for the Entra Connect to Cloud Sync transition that begins in July 2026.

**Remediations (formerly Proactive Remediations)** remain license-gated to Windows Enterprise E3/E5, Education A3/A5, M365 F3/E3/E5/A3/A5, or Windows VDA per-user. Detection plus remediation script pairs in PowerShell, exit-1-triggers-remediation pattern, 200 script packages and a 2,048-char output cap per device. Use them as the closed-loop for anomalies surfaced in Advanced Endpoint Analytics, and look at community libraries from oceanleaf.ch, andrewstaylor.com, and msendpointmgr.com for production-ready scripts (OneDrive KFM enforcement, BitLocker key-escrow checks, Defender exclusions audit, stale-profile cleanup).

For Graph automation, the modern stack is **Microsoft.Graph PowerShell SDK with your own registered Entra app authenticating by certificate** — the global "Microsoft Intune PowerShell" first-party client ID was removed April 1, 2024. Most Intune surfaces (Settings Catalog, Autopilot, ADE, Cloud PKI, EPM, Enterprise App Catalog, exportJobs) only exist on the `beta` endpoint, so plan to use `Microsoft.Graph.Beta.*` modules. Throttling caps: 100 requests per tenant per minute, 48 per app per minute, 8 per user per minute. The **old Microsoft.Graph.Intune module is deprecated**; AzureAD and MSOnline are retired. **Azure AD Graph (graph.windows.net) was fully retired January 31, 2025** — confirm no automation still calls it. The **Intune Data Warehouse beta Power BI connector retires in a rolling cutover starting April 20, 2026** — many tenants have legacy reports they don't realize are on the beta connector. Migrate to connector v2 or the OData feed.

The de facto policy-as-code stack remains community tooling — **IntuneManagement (Mikael Karlsson)** for export/import/compare in silent batch mode with app-reg + cert, **IntuneWin32App (Nickolaj Andersen)** for Win32 packaging in pipelines, **IntuneAssignmentChecker** for orphaned-assignment audits. The Microsoft-native Intune Configuration Policy export/import is useful for Settings Catalog but doesn't cover assignments, scope tags, or apps. For reporting beyond the 2-year native audit retention, ship Diagnostic Settings to a Log Analytics workspace (AuditLogs, OperationalLogs, DeviceComplianceOrg, Devices) and lean on the ugurkocde/KQL_Intune community queries.

In parallel, the operational hygiene items that pay continuous dividends: **migrate Administrative Templates to Settings Catalog, pilot Windows Autopilot device preparation for new Entra-join scenarios, roll out Platform SSO on Mac, eliminate the last Device Administrator-enrolled Android devices on GMS, deploy DDM-based Apple Software Update policies, enable Token Protection in CA for Windows, audit local admin rights against EPM dashboard data, and put the Intune Administrator role behind PIM**.

## Conclusion: the cloud-native end state has arrived

The shape of the work in mid-2026 reveals something larger than a list of features. **Microsoft's three-year campaign to consolidate endpoint management around Entra, Intune, and Defender has reached the point where every alternative path is either retired, deprecated, or actively discouraged**. Hybrid join, Administrative Templates, the AAD Graph endpoint, the old PowerShell modules, NDES for new deployments, MDM-based Apple software update commands, Android Device Administrator, the "Require approved client app" CA grant, and the standalone Intune Suite SKU for E5 customers are all on the way out within an eighteen-month window. What replaces them is uniformly cloud-native, uniformly authored through Settings Catalog or its DDM equivalent, and uniformly licensed through M365 bundles.

The strategic implication for an L3 team is that **the discretionary engineering effort available between now and 2027 is roughly fixed, and most of it should be spent on retirement work rather than new capabilities**. The teams that emerge from this year in good shape will have used the July 2026 repricing as the forcing function to retire third-party tooling that duplicates absorbed Suite components, pushed every new device build to Entra-join with Autopilot device preparation, rebuilt their Apple update enforcement on DDM, cleaned out their CA policies, and put Intune RBAC behind PIM with scope tags. The teams that emerge poorly will discover in 2027 that their MDE baseline is silently blocking BitLocker, their Power BI reports stopped refreshing, their CA exemptions stopped working, and they are paying for Cloud PKI, Patch My PC, and SCEPman simultaneously. The cliffs in the table above are not equally dangerous, but the calendar is unusually unforgiving — the unifying message of mid-2026 is that **operational debt is now a licensing decision as much as an engineering one**.
